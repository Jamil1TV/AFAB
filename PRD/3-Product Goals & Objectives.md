# 3. Product Goals & Objectives

## Purpose of this Section

This section defines what AFAB aims to achieve from three different perspectives:

* Business Goals
* User Goals
* Technical Goals

These objectives ensure that every feature developed contributes directly to the success of the product.

---

# 3.1 Business Goals

The business goals describe what AFAB aims to accomplish as a product and as a future SaaS platform.

## BG-01 — Build a Premium SaaS Product

Develop AFAB as a production-ready Software as a Service (SaaS) platform that follows modern software engineering practices and delivers a professional user experience.

---

## BG-02 — Simplify Business Finance Management

Provide an intuitive platform that allows business owners to manage their finances without requiring accounting expertise.

---

## BG-03 — Reduce Financial Complexity

Replace spreadsheets and fragmented tools with a centralized financial management system that simplifies daily operations.

---

## BG-04 — Establish a Scalable Foundation

Design the platform with scalability in mind so new modules, AI capabilities, and third-party integrations can be added without major architectural changes.

---

## BG-05 — Prepare for Commercial Deployment

Develop AFAB with production-quality architecture, security, and maintainability so it can evolve into a commercially viable SaaS product.

---

# 3.2 User Goals

These goals describe what users should accomplish using AFAB.

## UG-01 — Easily Track Financial Activities

Users should be able to record income and expenses quickly with minimal effort.

---

## UG-02 — Understand Financial Health

Users should gain a clear understanding of their business performance through dashboards, charts, and reports.

---

## UG-03 — Improve Budget Management

Users should be able to create budgets, monitor spending, and identify areas where costs exceed planned limits.

---

## UG-04 — Monitor Financial Progress

Users should be able to track savings goals, investment records, and overall business growth over time.

---

## UG-05 — Save Time

Reduce the time required to organize financial information and generate reports by providing a centralized and automated workflow.

---

## UG-06 — Feel Confident Using the Platform

Provide an interface that is simple enough for first-time users while remaining powerful enough for growing businesses.

---

# 3.3 Technical Goals

These goals define the engineering standards for AFAB.

## TG-01 — Clean Architecture

Follow SOLID principles and maintain a modular architecture that separates business logic, presentation, and data access.

---

## TG-02 — High Performance

Ensure fast loading times, optimized database queries, and responsive user interactions.

---

## TG-03 — Security

Protect user data through authentication, authorization, secure password storage, input validation, and industry-standard security practices.

---

## TG-04 — Maintainability

Write clean, readable, and well-documented code that supports future maintenance and feature development.

---

## TG-05 — Scalability

Design the backend and frontend to support increasing numbers of users, businesses, and financial records without requiring major restructuring.

---

## TG-06 — API-First Design

Expose all business functionality through a well-structured REST API, allowing future integrations with mobile applications and external services.

---

# 3.4 MVP Objectives

The first release of AFAB will focus on validating the core product by delivering the following capabilities:

* Secure user authentication.
* Personal dashboard.
* Income management.
* Expense management.
* Budget management.
* Savings goals.
* Manual investment tracking.
* Reports and analytics.
* Notifications.
* User profile and settings.

Artificial Intelligence features are intentionally excluded from Version 1.

---

# 3.5 Long-Term Objectives

Following a successful MVP, AFAB will expand to include:

* AI-powered financial insights.
* Smart expense categorization.
* Predictive budgeting.
* Cash-flow forecasting.
* Receipt OCR.
* Conversational financial assistant.
* Banking integrations.
* Multi-business management.
* Team collaboration.
* Mobile applications.

---

# 3.6 Success Indicators

The product will be considered successful when it achieves the following outcomes:

### Product Success

* Stable and reliable production-ready platform.
* Clean and scalable architecture.
* Positive user experience.

### User Success

* Users can manage finances efficiently.
* Users understand their financial position.
* Users reduce manual financial administration.

### Business Success

* AFAB is positioned for commercial SaaS deployment.
* The platform can support future subscription plans.
* The architecture is prepared for AI expansion and future growth.
