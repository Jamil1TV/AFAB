# 7. Target Audience & User Personas

## Purpose

This section defines who AFAB is built for. Understanding the target audience ensures that every product decision, feature, and user interface is designed around real user needs rather than assumptions.

The MVP focuses on individuals who own and manage a single small business and require a simple, modern solution for financial management.

---

# 7.1 Target Audience

## Primary Target Audience

AFAB is designed primarily for small business owners who manage their own finances and want a modern, easy-to-use platform without the complexity of traditional accounting software.

Typical users include:

* Coffee shops
* Restaurants
* Local retail stores
* Small service businesses
* Freelancers
* Consultants
* Agencies
* Online stores
* Startup founders
* Home-based businesses

These users often have limited accounting knowledge but need a clear understanding of their business finances.

---

## Secondary Target Audience

Although not the primary focus of the MVP, AFAB can also support:

* Independent professionals
* Side-business owners
* Self-employed individuals
* Small family businesses

These users benefit from the same financial management tools while operating on a smaller scale.

---

## Users Outside the MVP Scope

The following users are not the intended audience for Version 1:

* Large enterprises
* Corporations
* Government organizations
* Banks
* Professional accounting firms
* Businesses requiring ERP systems
* Businesses requiring payroll management
* Businesses requiring inventory management
* Teams with multiple finance employees

Support for these scenarios may be considered in future versions.

# 7.2 User Personas

## Persona 1 — Small Business Owner

**Name:** Ahmed

**Age:** 34

**Business:** Coffee Shop

### Background

Ahmed owns a neighborhood coffee shop with several daily income and expense transactions. He currently tracks finances using spreadsheets and handwritten notes.

### Goals

* Know how much profit the business is making.
* Keep expenses under control.
* Monitor monthly budgets.
* View business performance quickly.
* Save time on financial management.

### Pain Points

* Financial information is scattered.
* Manual calculations take too much time.
* Difficult to understand cash flow.
* Budget tracking is inconsistent.

### How AFAB Helps

AFAB provides Ahmed with one dashboard where he can monitor income, expenses, budgets, and reports in real time.

---

## Persona 2 — Freelancer

**Name:** Sarah

**Age:** 28

**Business:** Graphic Designer

### Background

Sarah manages freelance projects for multiple clients. She wants to organize income and expenses separately from her personal finances.

### Goals

* Track project income.
* Monitor monthly expenses.
* Save for future investments.
* Generate monthly financial reports.

### Pain Points

* Personal and business finances become mixed.
* Difficult to prepare financial summaries.
* Limited visibility into monthly earnings.

### How AFAB Helps

AFAB allows Sarah to manage all business-related finances from a single, organized platform.

---

## Persona 3 — Startup Founder

**Name:** Omar

**Age:** 31

**Business:** Technology Startup

### Background

Omar has recently launched a startup and wants to monitor financial growth without investing in expensive accounting software.

### Goals

* Track business growth.
* Monitor cash flow.
* Analyze spending patterns.
* Prepare reports for investors.
* Stay within budget.

### Pain Points

* Existing accounting software is expensive.
* Financial data is difficult to organize.
* Limited understanding of business performance.

### How AFAB Helps

AFAB gives Omar a modern financial dashboard with budgeting, reports, analytics, and performance tracking in one place.

---

# 7.3 Common User Needs

Across all target users, AFAB aims to satisfy the following needs:

* Fast financial data entry.
* Simple navigation.
* Clear dashboards.
* Reliable financial records.
* Budget monitoring.
* Progress tracking.
* Secure access.
* Responsive performance.
* Professional reporting.

---

# 7.4 Product Alignment

Every feature introduced into AFAB should answer the following question:

**Does this feature make financial management easier for our target users?**

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered or postponed.

## 7.5 Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary Ideal Customer

AFAB is primarily designed for **small business owners** who want a simple, modern, and affordable way to manage their business finances without the complexity of traditional accounting software.

### Profile

**Business Size**

* 1–20 employees

**Business Stage**

* Startup
* Early-stage business
* Growing small business

**Industries**

* Retail
* Cafés & Restaurants
* Service Businesses
* Freelancers
* Agencies
* E-commerce Stores
* Consultants
* Local Businesses

### Financial Management Style

The ideal customer currently manages finances using one or more of the following:

* Microsoft Excel
* Google Sheets
* Notes
* Basic finance applications
* Manual calculations
* Paper records

### Characteristics

The ideal customer:

* Is not a professional accountant.
* Wants a clear overview of business finances.
* Needs quick access to financial information.
* Values simplicity over complexity.
* Prefers visual dashboards and charts.
* Wants to make better financial decisions.
* Is open to adopting modern cloud-based software.

### Pain Points

* Spending too much time organizing financial records.
* Difficulty tracking business performance.
* Limited visibility into cash flow.
* Poor budget control.
* Financial data spread across multiple tools.
* Existing accounting software feels overly complex or expensive.

### Goals

The ideal customer wants to:

* Understand business performance instantly.
* Record income and expenses efficiently.
* Stay within budget.
* Monitor financial growth.
* Generate reports with minimal effort.
* Prepare for future business expansion.

### Why AFAB?

AFAB delivers exactly what this customer needs:

* A clean and intuitive interface.
* Centralized financial management.
* Actionable dashboards.
* Budget tracking.
* Financial reporting.
* A scalable platform that will evolve with AI-powered capabilities in future versions.
